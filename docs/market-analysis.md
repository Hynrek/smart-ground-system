# Smart Ground — Market Analysis & Positioning
### Clay Shooting Ranges: CH · DE · AT · IT · FR
*Stand: Juli 2026. Figures marked (est.) are estimates; sourced figures are linked at the bottom.*

---

## 1. Market Size (Addressable Ranges)

| Country | Ranges / Clubs | Confidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🇫🇷 France | **~568 clubs**, ~23,000 licensed (FFBT) | Sourced | Largest market by far. Strong ball-trap culture, many small rural clubs. |
| 🇮🇹 Italy | ~250–300 impianti (est.), 160+ confirmed active | Partial | FITAV-affiliated; strong competition culture (world-leading in Olympic trap). |
| 🇩🇪 Germany | ~300–500 Wurfscheibenstände (est.) | Estimate | No central registry. Split between DSB sport clubs and jagdliche Schiessanlagen (DJV Landesverbände). Every Jägerprüfung requires Flintenschiessen → steady base demand. |
| 🇨🇭 Switzerland | ~20 dedicated clay clubs + jagdliche Anlagen with Wurfscheiben (~40–60 total, est.) | Sourced/est. | Small but wealthy; home market, best for pilots. |
| 🇦🇹 Austria | ~20–40 (est.) | Estimate | VJWÖ organizes jagdliche competitions; small market. |

**Total addressable: roughly 1,000–1,500 ranges.** At an average of ~8–15 machines per range, that's an installed base of ~10,000–20,000 throwing machines — nearly all of which have **no connectivity**.

Key structural fact: this is a **retrofit market**, not a greenfield market. Machines (Laporte, Promatic, Mattarelli, Due Matic, Nasta…) last 15–25 years. Nobody replaces a working machine to get software.

## 2. Competitive Landscape

Three distinct groups — and none of them does what Smart Ground does end-to-end.

### A. Machine manufacturers with control add-ons (the real competitors)
| Player | Offering | Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| **Promatic (UK)** — *Claymate / Wi-Card* | Clay counting + release via contactless cards; cost-based, count-up/down modes. Promatic claims grounds increased revenue **up to 40%** through usage control. | Card/terminal-based, offline island. No cloud, no shooter app, no stats, no competition management. Capex sale. Strongest in UK/US. |
| **Laporte (FR)** | Radio controllers, dominant machine brand in FR/EU, Olympic supplier | Controllers are per-machine accessories; no platform. |
| **E.P. Progetti / Elettronica Progetti (IT)** | Chip-card charging stations, mic junction boxes, range electronics for FITAV-style ranges | Wired, installation-heavy, competition-range focus; no SaaS, no app. |
| **Canterbury / BRB / UK Release** | Voice-release units (~USD 1,700–4,000 per field) | Single-purpose release hardware; no counting, no platform. |

### B. Scoring & club software (apps only, mostly US)
HotClays, Clays Buddy, ClayTrack, Score Chaser, Klayscore, ClayResults — scoring, leaderboards, tournament registration. **None controls hardware.** Scores are entered manually on phones. Weak EU presence, English-only.

### C. Range technology integrators (DE: Johannsen, Rangemaster, qmax)
Sell and install machines + wiring per project. Potential **channel partners**, not competitors — they have the customer relationships but no software product.

### The gap
Nobody in Europe offers: **machine-agnostic retrofit hardware + cloud platform + shooter app + usage-based pricing** in one product. Claymate is the closest and it validates the core thesis (counting clays = more revenue for the owner), but it's a 2000s-era card system.

## 3. Positioning: "The operating system for clay ranges"

The market is saturated with *machines* and *release buttons* — it is empty of *platforms*. Don't position against machine makers; position **on top of them**.

### Core positioning statement
> Smart Ground retrofits any throwing machine, from any manufacturer, into a connected range — with per-clay billing, live scoring, and competition management. Pay per clay thrown, not per seat.

### The base promise
> **The more people shoot, the more we both earn.** Machine vendors get paid once and disappear; Smart Ground's revenue only grows when the range's does. Every feature that brings shooters back — leaderboards, training history, group play, faster throughput — grows the owner's round revenue and our per-clay fee in lockstep. No shooting, (almost) no cost: seasonal ranges pay next to nothing in winter.

### Why this wins
1. **Vendor-neutral retrofit.** Claymate works best inside the Promatic ecosystem; ranges have mixed fleets. "Works with your existing Laporte/Mattarelli/Promatic machines" removes the biggest objection (capex on machines already paid for).
2. **PayPerClay mirrors the industry's own economics.** Everything at a range is already per-clay (clay purchase ~9–13 Rp, shooter pays per round). A per-clay fee is the only pricing model owners intuitively accept — competitors sell capex boxes.
3. **Promatic's own marketing is your sales pitch.** "Up to 40% revenue increase from controlling and recording clay usage" — quote it. You deliver the same control plus stats, no-bird tracking per machine, payment tracking, and competition management on top.
4. **The shooter side is the moat.** No hardware competitor has a shooter identity: training history, Serien, leaderboards, group play. Once shooters carry their profile between grounds, ranges get pulled in ("do you have Smart Ground?"). Apps (group B) have the features but no hardware; you have both.

### Competition management: pricing & the acquisition flywheel
Competition value is event-shaped, not volume-shaped — price it per event, not per clay:

- **Club-internal events** (Clubmeisterschaft, Endschiessen): included in base abo. Retention: the club's championship history lives in Smart Ground.
- **Open/hosted tournaments**: per-starter fee CHF 2–3 (invisible vs. CHF 80+ entry fees; scales with event success). Covers online registration, squad planning, live scoring, clubhouse-TV leaderboards, published results — all already built.
- **Year one: waive the fee.** Every open competition is a customer-acquisition event: guest shooters register via free Smart Ground accounts, take their stats home, and pressure their home ranges ("why don't we have this?").

**The flywheel:** hosted competition → guest accounts → shooters pressure home ranges → new range integrates → its competitions onboard more shooters. Each new range makes every account more valuable (cross-range rankings, more events, comparable stats). Cold-start plan: land SM Compak + Goldene Taube at Hegen as season-one showcases. Accounts already have single-range value (score tracking, training history, custom Serien) — competitions are on top, not the foundation. **Second viral loop:** group play with custom Serien requires friends to create accounts — every training evening is a micro-acquisition event. Key metric: **guest-account activation rate** per event.

Guardrail: shooter basics (scores, history, custom Serien) stay free permanently; later shooter-side monetization only adds *new* premium value (advanced analytics, cross-range rankings) — never paywalls the basics retroactively.

### Smart Ground Universe (shooter platform, hardware-independent)
Free shooter website/app for stats tracking that works at **any** range, including non-connected ones (manual score upload). Decouples platform growth from hardware installs.

- **Verified vs. self-reported:** hardware-recorded scores carry a verified badge; manual uploads don't. Leaderboards rank verified scores → shooters lobby their ranges to install hardware so their scores "count". The manual tier is the demand generator for the connected tier.
- **One device instead of two:** shooters can "set up" their local unconnected range in Universe and use it as a score tracker (release remote + phone). At a Smart Ground range, the release device *is* the scorecard — scoring becomes a byproduct of shooting. Shooter-created range setups (Plätze, Serien, disciplines) double as pre-configuration: when the range installs hardware, its config and Serien already exist. Same Serie/Passe/Play schema for manual and connected; claimed (Tier 1) ranges canonicalize duplicate shooter setups.
- **Range funnel:** Tier 0 crowdsourced directory entry → Tier 1 owner claims page via code (schedule, info, visitor stats — free) → Tier 2 connected (hardware). Each tier is a natural upsell step.
- **Sales intelligence:** upload data shows which unconnected ranges have active Universe shooters → prioritized, warm sales targets.
- **Competitive edge vs. US scoring apps** (HotClays, Clays Buddy, ClayTrack): European disciplines (Jagdparcours, Compak, jagdliches Trap), German-first, and the only platform with a hardware-verified path.

### Go-to-market sequence
1. **CH first** (~10–20 realistic targets): home market, pilots, references. Small market is an advantage — you can reach density (cross-ground leaderboards work with 5 ranges in CH).
2. **DE second**: jagdliches Schiessen segment (Jägerprüfung traffic = predictable volume), enter via Landesjagdverband references and integrators (Johannsen etc.) as channel.
3. **FR/IT later**: biggest markets but require local presence, language, and FFBT/FITAV relationships. Italy is defended terrain (Progetti relationships, competition-grade requirements). Consider distribution partnerships over direct sales.

### Segment focus
Prioritize **commercial/jagdliche ranges (4+ Plätze)** over small volunteer clubs: they have payroll pain (unattended operation), theft/shrinkage pain (uncounted clays), and throughput pain — exactly what PayPerClay + automation solves. Small clubs follow later via a lighter bundle.

### What to defuse in sales conversations
- "Internet on a rural range?" → offline buffering + sync (already the architecture: Hub on-site).
- "What if you disappear?" → range keeps working locally; only cloud services degrade.
- "Per-clay fee forever?" → frame vs. shooter price: 2 Rp on a CHF 12–15 round is <0.5% of revenue, and the count itself recovers multiples of that in shrinkage alone (per Promatic's 40% claim).

## 4. Pricing Model (recap of current thinking)

- Boxes **sold** (CHF 500), Hub + Nodes **rented** → clean service termination, no repossession fights.
- **Base fee** CHF 50–100/month (Service, OTA, box replacement, support) → covers low-volume ranges.
- **PayPerClay 2 Rp** baseline; **5 Rp during financing** with a minimum monthly repayment (caps worst-case payback, no interest complexity).
- Shooter accounts free & platform-global (network effect); shooter-side monetization only after multi-ground density.
